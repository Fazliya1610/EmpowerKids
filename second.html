Child Rights

Understanding Child Rights

Child rights are the human rights of children with particular attention to the rights of special protection and care afforded to minors. These rights are fundamental freedoms and the inherent rights of all human beings below the age of 18.

Key Child Rights


  • Right to life, survival, and development



  • Right to education


Right to life, survival, and development

The "Right to Life, Survival, and Development" is one of the most fundamental and crucial rights that every child holds. It encompasses a range of essential provisions designed to ensure the well-being and potential of every child from birth and beyond. This right recognizes and guarantees a child's intrinsic value and the need for their physical, emotional, and intellectual growth.
