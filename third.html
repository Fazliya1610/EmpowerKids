Malala Yousafzai (born 1997)

Field:

Education and Activism

Biography:

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani human rights advocate known for her activism in promoting education for girls in Pakistan and around the world. She gained international recognition after surviving an assassination attempt by the Taliban at the age of 15. Malala has since become a global symbol of the fight for girls' education and has received numerous awards, including the Nobel Peace Prize.



Muniba Mazari (born 1987)

Field:

Social Activist

Biography:

Muniba Mazari, born in 1987 in Pakistan, is an artist, television host, and social activist. A life-altering car accident in 2007 left her paralyzed from the waist down, but she transformed her adversity into inspiration through her art, advocacy for disability rights, and motivational speaking. As Pakistan's first UNDP Goodwill Ambassador and a prominent figure in the arts and social activism, she continues to inspire and uplift others worldwide.



Kailash Satyarthi

Field:

Child Rights and Education

Biography:

Kailash Satyarthi is an Indian child rights activist who has dedicated his life to combating child labor and promoting children's rights to education. He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2014 for his work in rescuing children from forced labor and trafficking.



Amal Clooney

Field:

Human Rights and International Law

Biography:

Amal Clooney is a Lebanese-British lawyer and human rights activist who has worked on cases related to human rights abuses and international law violations. She has represented individuals and groups in various high-profile cases.



Shanta Sinha

Field:

Child Right Activist

Biography:

Shanta Sinha is an Indian child rights activist known for her work against child labor, particularly in the carpet industry. She founded the MV Foundation, which aims to eliminate child labor and promote education.



Emma Gonzalez (born 1999)

Field:

Activism

Biography:

Emma Gonzalez is a survivor of the 2018 Parkland school shooting in Florida, USA. She became an outspoken advocate for gun control and co-founded the student-led movement "Never Again MSD." Her passionate speeches and activism made her a prominent figure in the gun control debate.
